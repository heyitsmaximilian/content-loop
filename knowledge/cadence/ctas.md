## AVOID ⚠️

### Hard Sell
- **Text**: "Download Cadence now!"
- **Problem**: Too aggressive, breaks storytelling
- **Status**: Do not use

### Medical Claims CTA
- **Text**: "Optimize your GLP-1 results with Cadence"
- **Problem**: Implies Cadence affects medical outcomes — compliance violation
- **Status**: Do not use

### Fear/Urgency
- **Text**: "Don't miss another dose — get Cadence"
- **Problem**: Guilt-based, off-brand
- **Status**: Do not use

### Vague
- **Text**: "Check out my link in bio"
- **Problem**: No value proposition, no specificity
- **Status**: Do not use

## CTA Placement Strategy

### Slide 6 (Primary CTA)
- **Position**: Bottom third, clear against background
- **Context**: After showing the app solving the problem
- **Style**: Clean, matches app aesthetic (sage green on white)

### Slide 4-5 (Soft Integration)
- **Position**: App name visible in screen recording or screenshot
- **Context**: During product demonstration
- **Style**: The app itself IS the CTA — showing it in use

### Caption (Secondary)
- **Position**: End of post caption
- **Style**: "The app is Cadence — free to try on the App Store"
- **Context**: For users who read captions after watching

## A/B Testing Queue

### Test 1: Specificity vs Universality
- **Variant A**: "Built for people on GLP-1s" (niche)
- **Variant B**: "The easiest way to track your protocol" (broad)
- **Hypothesis**: Niche specificity converts better because audience self-selects

### Test 2: Action vs Benefit
- **Variant A**: "Search 'Cadence' on the App Store" (action)
- **Variant B**: "Get organized before your next appointment" (benefit)
- **Hypothesis**: Benefit framing converts better for health-conscious audience

### Test 3: Free vs Value
- **Variant A**: "Free to try" (removes friction)
- **Variant B**: "Worth every penny" (premium positioning)
- **Hypothesis**: "Free to try" wins on TikTok where impulse > consideration

## Compliance Rules for CTAs

- Never imply Cadence improves medical outcomes
- "Track" and "organize" are safe verbs; "optimize" and "improve results" are not
- "Free to try" is fine; "free health tool" needs careful framing
- App Store screenshots must not show fake medical data
