# Cadence — Brand Voice Guide

Cadence is a premium wellness app for people managing GLP-1 and peptide protocols. Dose tracking, symptom logging, metrics, progress insights, clinician-friendly exports.

**Brand position:** Premium wellness. Calm, modern, trustworthy. Not clinical, not biohacker.

**Target audience:** Adults on GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Mounjaro, Wegovy, tirzepatide, semaglutide) or peptide protocols. Mix of prescribed patients and self-directed users. Primarily 25-55, health-conscious, willing to pay for quality tools.

## Core Voice Attributes

### Calm Authority
- **Not**: Pushy health influencer with miracle claims
- **Not**: Cold clinical app with medical jargon
- **But**: Trusted companion on a health journey
- **Language**: "Track your progress", "See your patterns", "Stay on protocol"
- **Tone**: Confident but warm, like a knowledgeable friend

### Relatable Journey Partner
- **Not**: Expert giving medical advice
- **But**: Someone who gets the daily reality of managing a protocol
- **Language**: "We know the routine can be a lot", "Your data, organized"
- **Tone**: Empathetic, supportive, non-judgmental

### Quietly Premium
- **Not**: Flashy or hype-driven
- **But**: Clean, intentional, worth the investment
- **Language**: Simple words, no fluff, every word earns its place
- **Tone**: Understated confidence — the product speaks for itself

## Content Voice Framework

### Hook Voice (Slide 1-2)
**Purpose**: Connect through shared frustration of managing a protocol

**Template**:
> "[Relatable person] said [limiting belief about GLP-1/peptide management]"
> "I've been on [medication] for [time] and [common frustration]"

**Examples**:
> "My doctor asked how my Ozempic has been going and I had nothing to show her"
> "I've been on semaglutide for 3 months and I can't remember if I took Tuesday's dose"
> "My partner asked if the injections are actually working and I couldn't answer"

### Discovery Voice (Slide 3-4)
**Purpose**: Introduce Cadence as the obvious solution

**Template**:
> "Then I [found/started using] this app that [specific benefit]"
> "I wanted something that [need] without [common complaint about alternatives]"

**Voice Qualities**:
- Matter-of-fact, not breathless
- Focuses on the practical problem solved
- Honest about what it does and doesn't do
- Low-pressure discovery framing

### Transformation Voice (Slide 5-6)
**Purpose**: Show the concrete improvement

**Template**:
> "Now I [specific improved behavior] and [tangible outcome]"
> "My [doctor/clinician] actually said [positive reaction to organized data]"

**Voice Qualities**:
- Specific, measurable improvement (not vague "life-changing")
- Credibility through detail
- Calm satisfaction, not hype
- Natural invitation to try

## Emotional Journey Arc

### Start: Overwhelmed but Committed
- "I want to stay on protocol but it's hard to keep track"
- "I forget doses, lose track of symptoms, can't see if it's working"
- "My notes app is a mess of random numbers"
- Tone: Frustrated by the logistics, not the medication

### Middle: Organized and Confident
- "Everything's in one place now"
- "I can actually see the pattern"
- "My next appointment will be different"
- Tone: Relief, control regained

### End: Informed and Empowered
- "I showed my doctor my progress report and she was impressed"
- "I know exactly what's working and what to adjust"
- "I finally feel like I'm managing this, not just surviving it"
- Tone: Quiet confidence, pride in self-management

## Language Patterns

### Use These Phrases
- **Tracking**: "Log your dose", "Track your progress", "See your trends"
- **Insight**: "Your data shows", "Patterns over time", "Week-over-week"
- **Control**: "Stay on protocol", "Never miss a dose", "Your health, organized"
- **Export**: "Share with your doctor", "Clinician-ready reports", "Clean summaries"
- **Premium**: "Designed for this", "Built for people on protocol", "Worth it"

### Avoid These Phrases
- **Medical claims**: "Lose weight faster", "Improve your results", "Optimize your protocol"
- **Fear/guilt**: "Don't miss another dose", "You're falling behind", "Missing doses is dangerous"
- **Biohacker bro**: "Stack", "optimize", "hack your health", "protocol engineering"
- **Hype**: "Game-changer", "Revolutionary", "The only app you need"
- **Pressure**: "Limited time", "Act now", "Before it's too late"

## Compliance Requirements

**Critical — every piece of content must follow these rules:**
- No medical advice, diagnosis, or prescribing language
- Observational language only ("tracks", "logs", "shows" — never "recommends", "prescribes", "diagnoses")
- No causal claims ("Cadence helps you lose weight" = WRONG)
- Always position as a tracking/organization tool, not a treatment tool
- When mentioning medications, never imply Cadence affects their efficacy

## Platform Adaptations

### TikTok
- More personal, story-driven
- "I've been on Ozempic for 6 months and this is how I finally got organized"
- Shorter text, more visual demonstration
- Show the app in use (screen recordings, lifestyle shots)

### Instagram
- More polished, aspirational wellness aesthetic
- Clean screenshots, progress charts, before/after organization
- Carousel format: problem → solution → features → CTA

### X/Twitter
- More direct, information-dense
- "If you're on GLP-1s and still tracking doses in your notes app, there's a better way"
- Thread format: problem, feature highlights, user quote, CTA
